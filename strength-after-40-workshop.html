Slide 04

Why Lifting Changes The Body

  • 1Strength rises through neural adaptation first, then muscle growth over time.
  • 2Muscle fibres get larger with progressive overload.
  • 3Gains show up in daily tasks: stairs, carrying loads, walking.
  • 4The overload principle: the body only changes when challenged beyond usual demand.
Say The body is adaptive. Demand nothing and it shrinks capacity. Demand force safely and it builds it.

A Better Metabolism

  • •Preserves or builds lean mass during aging and weight loss.
  • •Lean mass is tied to resting metabolic rate, which falls with age.
  • •Helps counter age-related metabolic slowdown.
  • •Improves blood sugar control and insulin action.

Want easier weight control and energy? You need more active muscle, not just more cardio.

Why Women Need It
Even More After 40

  • •After menopause, bone loss speeds up and muscle decline becomes more concerning.
  • •These shifts raise risk of osteoporosis, weakness and lost function.
  • •Strength training gives the body a reason to hold on to tissue.

Slide 11 — Start Here

What To Start With After 40

Frequency

Train 2–3 times a week on non-consecutive days.

Movements

Squat, hinge, push, pull, carry and core stability.

Volume

1–3 sets of controlled reps with gradual overload.

Priority

Form, joint comfort and consistency over heavy lifting.

Say Think squats, lunges, rows, chest press, lat pulldown and basic carries, scaled to the person in front of you.

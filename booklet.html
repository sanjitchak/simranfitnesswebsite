Why This Matters

Your body was not built to sit for 8 to 10 hours.

Most office health problems do not start loudly. They build quietly through small daily habits.

What long desk hours can create

  • Low energy after lunch
  • Neck, back, and shoulder stiffness
  • Weight gain from low movement and stress eating
  • Poor sleep from late screens and mental overload
  • Lower focus during meetings and deep work

The good news

You do not need a perfect routine. You need a few useful habits done consistently.

  • Move for a few minutes
  • Eat enough protein
  • Reduce long sitting blocks
  • Sleep a little better
  • Track your progress simply

Today’s simple promise

You will leave this workshop with one desk routine, one food rule, one energy score, and one 7 day action plan.

Desk Reset

The 5 minute office movement routine

Use this routine once in the morning and once after lunch. Move slowly. Stop if you feel pain.

60sec

Neck reset

Look left and right slowly. Then tilt ear toward shoulder on both sides. Keep the shoulders relaxed.

60sec

Shoulder circles

Roll shoulders backward 10 times, then forward 10 times. Open the chest after each roll.

60sec

Seated spine twist

Sit tall. Turn gently to one side, hold, breathe, then repeat on the other side.

60sec

Hip wake up

Stand up. March in place. Lift knees slowly. Keep breathing steady.

60sec

Sit to stand

Sit down and stand up 8 to 12 times. Use a chair. Keep the movement controlled.

Your next posture is your best posture.4
Movement Snacks

Break long sitting without leaving work.

You do not need one perfect workout to feel better. You need small movement breaks across the day.

1

Every 60 minutes

Stand up for 2 minutes. Walk, stretch, or do 10 chair squats.

2

After lunch

Walk for 5 to 10 minutes. This helps digestion and reduces the afternoon crash.

3

Before meetings

Open your chest, roll shoulders, and take 5 slow breaths.

4

Phone calls

Take walking calls when possible. It adds steps without extra time.

5

Lift safely

When picking something up, bend from hips and knees, not only the back.

6

Start small

Aim for 6,000 to 8,000 steps if you are currently very inactive.

Food Reset

Eat for energy, not punishment.

Most working professionals do not need a strict diet first. They need meals that keep energy stable.

Half plate: vegetables or salad

Fiber keeps you fuller and supports digestion.

Quarter plate: protein

Paneer, dal, curd, eggs, chicken, fish, tofu, sprouts, or lean meat.

Quarter plate: carbs

Rice, roti, poha, oats, potato, fruit, or other simple home foods.

Add water

Many people confuse thirst with hunger or fatigue.

Simple office snack swaps

Tea and biscuits to tea with roasted chana
Chips to fruit and curd
Sugary drink to water or lemon water
Random snacking to planned protein snack
Do not eat less first. Eat better first.6
Afternoon Crash

Why you feel sleepy after lunch

The afternoon crash is common. But it is not normal to feel useless after every lunch.

Common causes

  • Very heavy carb meal with little protein
  • Low water intake
  • No walking after lunch
  • Poor sleep the night before
  • Too much caffeine and too little real food

Quick fix plan

  • Add protein to lunch
  • Drink water before coffee
  • Walk for 5 to 10 minutes after eating
  • Keep dinner lighter if you sleep late
  • Avoid using sugar as an energy solution

Stress And Sleep Reset

Your body recovers when your nervous system calms down.

Stress does not only live in the mind. It shows up in cravings, poor sleep, body tightness, and low patience.

1

Breathing reset

Inhale for 4 counts. Exhale for 6 counts. Repeat for 2 minutes before sleep or meetings.

2

Screen cut down

Reduce bright screen use during the last 30 minutes before sleep.

3

Brain dump

Write tomorrow’s top 3 tasks before bed. This helps the mind switch off.

4

Light dinner

Avoid going to bed too full. Heavy meals can disturb sleep.

5

Caffeine timing

Try to keep caffeine earlier in the day if sleep quality is poor.

6

Walk it out

A 10 minute walk can clear stress better than scrolling for 30 minutes.
